# Crown & Conjure

A prompt builder for Black hair in fantasy worlds.

[**▶ Live site**](https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/crown-and-conjure/) *(replace with your URL after deploying)*

## What this is

A web-based prompt builder that helps users generate AI fantasy concept art featuring Black characters with accurate, culturally specific hairstyles. It pairs a curated dictionary of 20 Black hairstyles (locs, Fulani braids, bantu knots, cornrows, box braids, knotless braids, stitch braids, lemonade braids, Senegalese twists, Marley twists, two-strand twists, twist-outs, wash-and-gos, natural afros, afro puffs, high-top fades, finger waves, halo braids, goddess locs, and faux locs) with fantasy archetypes (warrior, mage, royal, ranger) and aesthetic frames (Afrofuturism, Africanfuturism, dark fantasy, mythic, non-European high fantasy).

The output is formatted differently depending on which AI tool you're using — Midjourney, DALL-E / ChatGPT, or Stable Diffusion / generic.

## Why it exists

AI image generators are trained on the open web, which means they inherit its biases. Default outputs skew white, straight-haired, and Eurofantasy. To get Black characters with accurate hair, users have to add qualifiers, name specific styles, fight the model's defaults, and often regenerate many times. This is unpaid labor — the cost of being a non-default user.

Sociologist Ruha Benjamin calls this kind of pattern *"the New Jim Code"*: new technologies that "reflect and reproduce existing inequities but that are promoted and perceived as more objective and progressive than the discriminatory systems of a previous era." This tool is a small intervention at the prompt-engineering layer.

## Lineage

This project stands on the shoulders of:

- **A.M. Darke**'s [Open Source Afro Hair Library](https://afrohairlibrary.org/) — free 3D models of Black hair, by Black artists.
- **Code My Crown** — Dove + OSAHL's [free 200-page guide](https://www.dove.com/us/en/campaigns/purpose/code-my-crown.html) for coding textured Black hair in games.
- **Amira Virgil (Xmiramira)**'s Melanin Pack and **The Black Simmer** community.
- **Nnedi Okorafor**'s framing of *Africanfuturism* — distinct from Afrofuturism, rooted in continental African cultures.
- **Woodrow Winchester**'s argument for Afrofuturism as a "design lens."

## Use

Open the live site, pick a hairstyle, archetype, aesthetic, and skin tone, choose your target tool (Midjourney / DALL-E / Stable Diffusion), and copy the generated prompt. Paste into your tool of choice. Generate at least 4 outputs — the first one rarely captures cultural specificity, since AI is stochastic. Iterate.

## A note on authority

This is a starting point, not a definitive reference. Hairstyle origins and cultural context deserve careful sourcing — the descriptions here are deliberately brief and should be cross-checked against community sources (Code My Crown, Ayana Byrd & Lori Tharps's *Hair Story: Untangling the Roots of Black Hair in America*, and the work of A.M. Darke). Corrections and additions welcome.
